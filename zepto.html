Zepto AOV Optimization

From Urgency Orders to Basket Expansion

The Problem Context

Zepto was experiencing strong order growth, but low Average Order Value (AOV). Users were driven by small, urgency-driven orders and typically stopped once they hit the free delivery threshold. This limited category exploration and capped revenue.

Detailed Problem Statements

The Zepto Paradox: Users currently place small, urgency-driven orders because the product is optimized for speed, not discovery, which results in low AOV.

Multiple Delivery Slots: Users currently hesitate to add multiple items to their orders because they expect a single 10-minute delivery and fear that adding more items may lead to split or delayed deliveries, which results in smaller basket sizes and lower AOV.

Behavioral Assumptions & Blockers

Research indicated users had a "mission-complete" mindset. They weren't looking to browse; they just wanted their immediate needs met. The primary blockers preventing basket expansion were structural, psychological, and experiential.

The UX Break Point: The Cart

The cart page was heavily optimized for quick checkout rather than basket building. Once users hit the threshold, they abandoned exploration. The "Zepto Paradox" was that the platform's optimization for speed was actively hurting AOV.

The Solution: Mission-Based Cart Expansion

To fix this, the product framing needed to shift from "Buy Item" to "Complete Basket". We proposed creating dedicated category bundles and "Add without delay" badges in the cart to reassure users that adding items wouldn't break their 10-minute SLA.

Prioritization & KPIs

Using the RICE framework, Mission-based cart expansion scored the highest priority due to its massive reach and direct targeting of the cart break point. We established primary KPIs like AOV increase and attach rate, while setting guardrails to protect checkout conversion.
