Zepto AOV Optimization

From urgency-driven basket to exploration-first shopping — increasing average order value without breaking speed perception.

The Zepto Paradox

Zepto was experiencing strong order growth, but low Average Order Value (AOV). Users were driven by small, urgency-driven orders and typically stopped once they hit the free delivery threshold.

💡

Users place small orders because the product is optimized for speed, not discovery — which results in low AOV and limits revenue growth.

Behavioral Assumptions & Blockers

Research indicated users had a 'mission-complete' mindset. They weren't looking to browse; they just wanted their immediate needs met. The primary blockers preventing basket expansion were structural, psychological, and experiential.

AOV Optimization Strategies

I proposed a multi-layered intervention: discovery-first homepage redesign, smart cross-sell triggers, and a basket-value nudge that reframes delivery thresholds as savings opportunities.

Metrics & Success Framework

Defining the north-star metric, guardrail metrics, and experiment design to validate the AOV lift without harming core speed perception.
