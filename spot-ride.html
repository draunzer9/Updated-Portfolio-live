SPOT Ride: Solving Pickup Confusion

Solving the last-meter problem in ride-hailing through visual coordination and intelligent pickup point selection.

The Widespread Confusion

Users and drivers face widespread confusion over pin placement and multi-entrance locations. Internal studies confirmed ~4% of ride requests have large GPS drift, and a staggering 42% of those rides are cancelled.

💡

Location Ambiguity: GPS provides ±50–100m accuracy. Pickup pins map to the wrong gate or nearby road instead of the exact point. "Arrived" does not equal visually findable.

Target Market & Constraints

This problem severely impacts 5–8M core daily commuters in dense urban areas, airports, and gated communities. External constraints like traffic rules, no-stopping zones, and one-way roads compound the difficulty.

Key Findings & Workarounds

GPS accuracy is completely insufficient for the final 'last-meter' rendezvous. Users resort to unstructured phone calls, manual pin adjustments, or simply walking—none of which are optimal UX.

The Solution: Pinpoint Meet Buddy

I conceptualized and designed a prototype replacing unstructured calls with visual, contextual cues — offering step-by-step last-meter guidance and auto-selecting optimal pickup points based on real-world feasibility.

💡

Auto-selected pickup points reduce cancellation rates by eliminating GPS ambiguity at the last 100 meters.
