SPOT Ride

Solving Pickup Confusion

The Widespread Confusion

Users and drivers face widespread confusion over pin placement and multi-entrance locations. Internal studies confirmed ~4% of ride requests have large GPS drift, and a staggering 42% of those rides are cancelled.

Detailed Problem Statement

Location Ambiguity: GPS provides ±50-100m accuracy. Pickup pins map to the wrong gate or nearby road instead of the exact point. "Arrived" does not equal visually findable.

Communication Gaps: High reliance on calls and manual instructions creates language barriers, time delays, and misinterpretation of landmarks.

Coordination Issues: Rider and driver operate with different location views and constraints (e.g. one-way roads, no-stopping zones), leading to inevitable confusion.

Target Market & Constraints

This problem severely impacts 5-8M core daily commuters in dense urban areas, airports, and gated communities. External constraints like traffic rules, no-stopping zones, and one-way roads compound the difficulty of accurate pickups.

Key Findings & Workarounds

We discovered that GPS accuracy (±50-100m) is completely insufficient for the final "last-meter" rendezvous. Users resort to unstructured phone calls ("Come near the gate"), manual pin adjustments, or simply walking to easier points—none of which are optimal UX.

The Solution: Pinpoint Meet Buddy

I conceptualized and designed a prototype to deliver better pickup guidance. It replaces unstructured calls with visual, contextual cues, offering step-by-step last-meter guidance and auto-selecting optimal pickup points based on real-world feasibility rather than raw GPS data.
