Acquisition — Where X Wins
X dominates public discourse. Viral screenshots, quoted tweets, and embedded posts generate massive inbound traffic from outside the platform. This is the engine's fuel — but it's leaking badly at the next stage.
🔑 Key Finding: External traffic via viral screenshots is NOT converting to signups. Users view content through embeds and never hit the registration wall.

Activation — The Broken Moment
New users land on an unfiltered, chaotic feed full of strangers. The "aha moment" — finding exactly the content you care about — is delayed by days. Most users churn before they ever feel the platform's value.
🔑 Key Finding: Immediate feed confusion and information overload delays the user's "aha moment," causing over 58% early churn within the first session.

Retention — Trust & Toxicity
Both creators and power users report algorithmic distrust — they cannot predict what content gets amplified. Toxicity in replies compounds this, making sharing feel risky. Premium subscribers churn at disproportionate rates.
🔑 Key Finding: Algorithm opacity is a top churn driver. Users don't understand WHY specific content appears, destroying the feedback loop needed to make the platform feel "like mine."

X Premium adoption remains low. Users cannot clearly articulate the value of the subscription relative to the free tier. The paywall on features feels arbitrary rather than earned through platform value.
